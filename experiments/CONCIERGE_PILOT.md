# RepMint Concierge Camera-Coaching Pilot

## Purpose

Test the RepMint coaching loop before building the full AI app:

1. Choose goal.
2. Do a short planned workout.
3. Use camera observation.
4. Count reps and estimate TUT/tempo.
5. Give one live cue at a time.
6. Send a short set/session review.
7. Ask whether the user would repeat it.

## Participant Criteria

Recruit 8-12 participants who:

- Train at least once per week.
- Train alone at least sometimes.
- Are comfortable doing simple bodyweight movements.
- Understand this is product research, not medical advice.

Do not recruit people looking for injury, rehab, or medical guidance.

## Consent And Privacy Script

Use this before the session:

> Thanks for helping test RepMint. This is a product research session for an AI camera training companion. We are testing whether goal-based plans, camera feedback, rep counting, time under tension, and set review feel useful.
>
> This is not medical or safety guidance. Please only do movements you already feel comfortable doing, stop whenever you want, and skip anything that does not feel right for you.
>
> We will only use your session notes for product research. If recording is used, we will ask first. You can ask us to delete notes or recordings at any time.

## Pre-Session Questions

1. What is your current training goal?
2. How often do you work out?
3. Where do you usually train?
4. What equipment do you have today?
5. What do you most want help with: plan, form, reps, tempo, motivation, progress, or something else?
6. Are there any movements you want to skip today?

## Session Options

Choose one based on participant comfort.

### Option A: Live Observation

- Use Zoom, Google Meet, FaceTime, or in-person observation.
- Participant positions phone/laptop so full body is visible.
- Team member acts as the RepMint coach.
- Give short cues only.
- Count reps and estimate tempo/TUT manually.

### Option B: Recorded Sample

- Participant records 1-3 short sets.
- Participant sends the video privately.
- Team sends a RepMint-style set review within 24 hours.
- Best when live scheduling is difficult.

## Pilot Workout Templates

### Template 1: Strength Foundation

- Squat: 2 sets of 8-12 reps.
- Push-up or incline push-up: 2 sets of 6-10 reps.
- Plank: 2 holds of 20-40 seconds.

### Template 2: Lower-Body Control

- Squat: 2 sets of 8-12 reps.
- Reverse lunge: 2 sets of 6-8 reps per side.
- Hip hinge drill: 2 sets of 8-10 reps.

### Template 3: Mobility And Control

- Hip hinge drill: 2 sets of 8 reps.
- Lunge mobility drill: 2 sets per side.
- Plank hold: 2 holds of 20-40 seconds.

## Live Coaching Rules

Use:

- Move with control.
- Slow the lowering phase.
- Keep your range consistent.
- Stay centered in frame.
- Good rep.
- Take a little more rest.
- Same pace on the next rep.

Avoid:

- Clinical language.
- Injury or safety claims.
- Perfect form claims.
- Diagnosis.
- Harsh judgment.
- More than one cue at a time.

## Manual Set Review Template

Send this after each set or session:

```text
RepMint set review

Movement:
Goal:
Reps/hold:
Estimated time under tension:
Tempo note:
What looked consistent:
Next focus:

Coach note:
[One short, practical sentence under 30 words.]
```

Example:

```text
RepMint set review

Movement: Squat
Goal: Strength foundation
Reps/hold: 10 reps
Estimated time under tension: 36 seconds
Tempo note: Lowering phase sped up near the end
What looked consistent: Rep depth stayed similar
Next focus: Control

Coach note:
Good set. Keep the lowering phase patient and use the same range on the next round.
```

## Post-Session Questions

Ask immediately after the workout:

1. How useful was the feedback? Rate 1-5.
2. How clear were the cues? Rate 1-5.
3. How comfortable was the camera setup? Rate 1-5.
4. Did the set review tell you what to focus on next?
5. Would you use this once per week if it were an app?
6. Which part mattered most: plan, camera cues, rep count, TUT/tempo, set review, or progress?
7. What felt annoying, unclear, or unnecessary?
8. What would you need to trust this product?
9. Would you join a second test?

## Data To Record

- Participant ID.
- Date.
- Goal.
- Training location.
- Movement template.
- Completed sets.
- Usefulness rating.
- Cue clarity rating.
- Camera comfort rating.
- Repeat intent.
- Top valued feature.
- Biggest objection.
- Quote from participant.
- Follow-up permission.

## Success Criteria

Strong signal:

- 8 completed sessions.
- Average usefulness rating of 4 or higher.
- Average cue clarity rating of 4 or higher.
- 50 percent or more would use it weekly.
- 3 or more users ask to join another test.

Weak signal:

- Users struggle to set up the camera.
- Users find cues obvious or distracting.
- Users prefer a normal workout plan with no camera feedback.
- Users do not want to repeat the experience.
